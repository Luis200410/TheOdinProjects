Empanadas are golden, crunchy corn turnovers filled with spiced beef and potatoes.

Colombian street-food favorite, usually dipped in ají picante sauce.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups precooked cornmeal (masarepa, like P.A.N. brand)
  • 2 ½ cups warm water
  • 1 tbsp oil
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 lb ground beef
  • potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tomato, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • >1 tsp cumin
  • >Salt and pepper to taste
  • Oil for frying
  1. Prepare the filling:
    • Boil potatoes until soft. Mash lightly.
    • In a pan, cook the beef with onion, garlic, tomato, cumin, salt, and pepper. Add potatoes and mix. Let it cool.
  2. Make the dough:
    • In a bowl, mix masarepa, salt, oil, and water. Knead until smooth.
  3. Assemble:
    • Take a small dough ball, flatten it between plastic sheets into a circle.
    • Add a spoonful of filling in the center, fold over, and press edges with a fork or fingers.
  4. Fry:
    • Heat oil in a deep pan (350°F / 180°C). Fry empanadas until golden and crisp (3 to 5 minutes).
  5. Drain on paper towels and serve with spicy ají sauce or lime.
