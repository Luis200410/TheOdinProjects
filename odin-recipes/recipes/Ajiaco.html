Ajiaco

Home

Ajiaco is a comforting chicken and potato soup from Bogotá,

rich and creamy with corn, capers, and a special herb called guasca. It’s perfect for cold days.

Ingredients

  • 2 chicken breasts (bone-in preferred)
  • 3 types of potatoes: 3 medium criollas (yellow), 3 pastusas, 3 sabaneras (if not available, just mix starchy + waxy potatoes)
  • 3 ears of corn, cut in halves
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tsp dried guasca (found in Latin stores)
  • 8 cups of water or chicken broth
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • ½ cup capers
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro (optional, for garnish)

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, bring water or chicken broth to a boil with chicken, onion, garlic, salt, and pepper. Cook until the chicken is tender (about 35–40 minutes).
  2. Remove the chicken, shred it, and set aside.
  3. Add the potatoes (peeled and cut) and corn. Cook until the potatoes start breaking down, thickening the soup (about 25–30 minutes).
  4. Add the guasca and let it simmer for 10 more minutes.
  5. Return the shredded chicken to the pot. Adjust seasoning.
  6. Serve hot with a spoon of cream and a few capers on top. Add rice and avocado on the side if you like.
